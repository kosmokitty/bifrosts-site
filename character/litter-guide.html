Mateships and Litters

Before pursuing a romance or litter, please read this guide thoroughly to better understand mateship and litter guidelines.

  • Cats should not consider dating until they are at least 12 cycles old.
  • Puppy crushing is allowed for kittens, but serious or romantic relations are not allowed at this stage.
  • Characters must be 21+ cycles in order to have a litter.
  • Be mindful of terminology used in the server; excessive descriptions of pregnancy should be spoilered.
  • Please do not roleplay characters giving birth in the server. If roleplayed privately and submitted for stardust, mark with a TW and spoilers.
  • Bifrosts allows a set amount of litters each season depending on city. Plan ahead as requested dates may not be available.
  • Litters can have 1–6 kittens. Characters may only have 16 kittens in their lifetime.
  • Staff reserves the right to refuse a litter for any reason.

Mateships

Dating

Dating in bifrosts does not come with a purchase, unless it requires an NPC. Characters can date whomever as long as it’s within our rules and regulations. Those who are dating will not be considered an “official couple” until they tie the knot and decide to get betrothed through purchasing items.​

Requirements:

Litters

Litters are fundamental in Bifrosts, run by staff for official openings and by members for character litters. They are one of the easiest ways to get into the group. Trying out for a litter is one thing, but what about pursuing a litter for your character? The process can get a little confusing but worry not as we’re here to help you through it.

Litter Requirements

Before opening a ticket, we ask that members have the requirements for the litter already complete! Each player must contribute one piece of art or literature worth 50 stardust. This can be a roleplay or a collaboration! Here’s what that looks like for each type:​

Literature

Each player contributes 750 words (1,500 total)

Artwork

Each player needs to artwork worth 50 stardust, which could be two full-bodies per person, or a collab that includes two-fullbodies, a moderate background, and shading.

Litter Tickets

Once requirements have been met, you will need to open a ticket with staff to further discuss the possibility of having a litter. This allows the team to look over who is involved as well as when you’re looking to have the litter and compare with our own timeline.

You may only open up a litter ticket two seasons in advance due to ticket and time constraints. If a ticket is opened beforehand we will ask you to reopen closer to the set timeframe for the litter.

Rolling Litters

If your litter has been approved, the staff team will roll the number of kittens, their genders, and any potential mutations.

Any mutations rolled at this point are free, and a roleplayer who gets a kitten with a rolled ban/mutation will not have to pay for it. Additionally, the mutation will not count towards a city’s cap for that mutation (which can be seen on our google-sheets spreadsheet). You are also not obligated to use the mutation if it is not something you want for your character, and it can be removed without any extra cost.

However, if you didn’t roll for a ban or mutation for your kitten but you would like it applied, it would need to be purchased and it will count towards a city’s cap for that very mutation.

Litter Adjustments

  • Extra kitten: 100 stardust
  • Remove kitten: free
  • Genetic reroll (one kitten): 100 stardust
  • Full litter reroll: 200 stardust

Bifrost litters may have 1-6 kittens per litter with a general maximum of 16 kittens in their lifetime in order to keep our family trees as neat as possible.

Example

  • Litter 1: 6 kittens
  • Litter 2: 6 kittens
  • Litter 3: 4 kittens
  • No fourth litter (max reached)

Additionally, if one of your kits dies before they reach 6 cycles, no matter the cause, you’ll get an extra slot tacked onto your next litter if you choose to have one. Or, due to recent rule changes, if a kitten dies because of inactivity it can either be NPCed or played by another character.

Hosting Tryouts

Members are free to decide how their kittens are adopted out, whether privately or through tryouts for current and new members. If you choose to host tryouts, you must send the staff team the tryout document link in a ticket so we can post it in the official tryouts channel. You may also post them on our DeviantArt page for extra visibility. Tryouts typically run for two weeks, with winners announced and kittens “born” the next day.

Before accepting your tryouts, ensure that the chosen roleplayer meets all Bifrosts’ requirements and is in the Discord server to complete the mandatory form. Staff will roll each kitten’s familiar after selection, and once kittens and their bios are approved and posted to DeviantArt, they may be roleplayed. Kittens cannot walk or talk until they are one cycle old, which will always occur on the first age-up after birth.

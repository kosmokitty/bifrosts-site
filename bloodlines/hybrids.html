Hybrids

Heterochromia is a hereditary genetic trait in which each iris differs in color. In Fyrstrheim, this isn't merely genetic expression, it is a mark of treachery. Those born with mismatched eyes are branded as Hybrids, a living curse upon their city.

To be a Hybrid is to bear the evidence of a tainted bloodline, one that has fraternized with the enemy or broken city laws. Across every city, Hybrids are seen as divine punishment: a sign from the gods that something in their lineage has gone terribly wrong.

Whenever misfortune strikes, Hybrids often become the scapegoats. They are accused of bringing curses upon the city, and depending on the offense or superstition of the moment, may be maimed, exiled, or executed. Though each city punishes differently, all share the same belief: a Hybrid is a blight that must be contained.

Note: While characters may experience this persecution in-character, discussions or portrayal of self-harm or suicide is prohibited. Provoking or suggesting these actions may violate community rules!

The Edicts Of The Cities

"Compassion for the corrupt is corruption itself."

While no city commands its people to take their own lives or the lives of others, openly aiding or showing sympathy towards a Hybrid is considered an act of defiance against divine order. Those who speak kindly of a Hybrid, defend them, or show them mercy risk being accused of sharing their curse.

Acts of Rebellion

Defending a hybrid often results in exile, imprisonment, or worse. This path requires discussion and permission from staff before it occurs.

Consequences

These aren't choices that should be made lightly: they are acts of rebellion that can shape the fate of an entire bloodline.

Hybrid Types

In Frystrheim, there are two distinct classifications of hybrids found within the world:

True Hybrid

Born within their home city to loyal bloodlines.

Their eyes mirror the city's color, but with an off hue: faintly distorted, as if touched by another paw.

Mixed Hybrid

Born of two bloodlines from opposing cities.

Their eyes reveal both ancestries, each iris bearing a different ancestry's color. These are proof of a union that should have never been.

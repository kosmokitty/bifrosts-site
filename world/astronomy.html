Astronomy

The skies above Fyrstrheim hold a quiet mystery. Two moons rise and fall in a dance older than the world itself. With them come stronger tides, more frequent tsunamis, heightened volcanic activity, and a multitude of earthquakes.

The constellations, shaped by the creatures and gods of this land, trace paths known only to those who walk the earth. Moonlight reflects not just upon the land, but upon the ancient forces of Fenrir and Jord, whose influence runs through the very stars.

Constellations and Stars

In The Song of the Seer, the origin of the stars and moons is woven into a tapestry passed down for generations — a tale of the world's birth and its foretold end at Ragnarök. Fyrstrheim was shaped from the fallen body of the great giant, Ymer, whose skull became the sky's dome above. Four ancient, sightless cats now hold it steady at the corners of the world. Sparks from Muspellheim leapt into the heavens and became the stars.

Ulf's Keptr

The Mouth of the Wolf — representing the wolves that hunt the sun and moon. Otherwise known as Pisces.

Friggs Distaff

Orion. Often used for navigation by those who live along the ocean.

Karlsvagn

Ursa Major — the Man's Chariot. Some believe it once belonged to Thor.

Kvennavagn

Ursa Minor — the Woman's Chariot. Often associated with Freya.

Auriga

Also called Ásar Bardagi — the battlefield of the gods, interpreted as Thor's struggle at Ragnarök.

Dagstjarna

The Day Star (Arcturus). Visible before sunrise in late winter and spring — a sign that dawn is coming.

Leidarstjarna

The Guiding Star (Polaris). Always in the north, and used for navigation.

Zodiak of Fyrstrheim

The cats of Fyrstrheim, religious as ever, often look to the stars for answers — and at times, the stars whisper back. Cycles ago, a cat known as Starri took on the mantle of Zodiak Keeper. She traveled to the cities and revealed how birth months dictate personality, beliefs, and actions.

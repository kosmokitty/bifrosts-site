Ailments

This page may often be used by those who roleplay Healing class characters in Bifrosts. To view an ailment, please click the box to see it's symptoms and treatment! More ailments can be added overtime. If you'd like to see specific ailments added, please let us know in the suggestion channel.

Bee & Wasp Stings

A painful nuisance often caused by curious kittens or hunting mishaps. Some cats may have allergic reactions.

Symptoms

Localized swelling, sharp pain, hives, difficulty breathing.

Treatment

Blackberry leaves pulp, mud cooling compress, wild garlic.

Blood Loss

Often follows deep wounds or battle injuries. Immediate care is required.

Symptoms

Pale gums, weakness, collapse, cold extremities.

Treatment

Cobweb binding, yarrow, enforced rest, warmth.

Broken Bones

A result of serious accidents. Minor fractures may heal with rest, while major breaks require binding and long-term care.

Symptoms

Intense local pain, limping, swelling, deformity.

Treatment

Cobweb binding, poppy seeds, willow bark, strict rest.

Chills

Associated with cold weather or prolonged exposure to water.

Symptoms

Low temperature, shivering, lethargy, unresponsiveness.

Treatment

Mint, feverfew, warmth, dry bedding.

Cracked Pads

Caused by long travel, harsh terrain, or extreme cold.

Symptoms

Dry paw pads, bleeding, pain when walking.

Treatment

Willow, wintergreen, reindeer moss, goldenrod.

Drowning

Occurs when lungs fill with water after falling into rivers or lakes.

Symptoms

Wet cough, weak breathing, exhaustion, confusion.

Treatment

Coltsfoot, warmth, strict rest, close monitoring.

Fever

An abnormally high body temperature, often caused by infection.

Symptoms

High temperature, chills, fatigue, delirium.

Treatment

Feverfew, borage, beech leaves, black alder.

Frostbite

Damage caused by prolonged exposure to freezing temperatures.

Symptoms

Pale pads, numbness, cracked skin, stiffness.

Treatment

Slow warming, willow bark, enforced rest.

Green Cough

A serious chest infection common in fall and winter.

Symptoms

High fever, persistent cough, wheezing, loss of appetite.

Treatment

Mint, feverfew, beech leaves, black alder.

Heatstroke

Common during high summer without access to shade or water.

Symptoms

Heavy panting, extreme lethargy, dehydration.

Treatment

Cool water soak, borage, wet moss hydration.

Joint Aches

Often seen in elders or during damp seasons.

Symptoms

Joint pain, stiffness, difficulty moving.

Treatment

Cinquefoil, goldenrod, reindeer moss, willow bark.

Parasites

Internal or external pests that drain strength over time if untreated.

Symptoms

Weight loss, dull fur, itching, weakness.

Treatment

Juniper berries, tansy, strict grooming, reindeer moss.

Smoke Inhalation

Damage to the lungs after exposure to fire and ash.

Symptoms

Raspy breathing, coughing, soot-covered fur, sore throat.

Treatment

Coltsfoot, honey, fresh air, enforced rest.

Concussion

A head injury caused by heavy impact or falling.

Symptoms

Disorientation, vomiting, imbalance, delayed responses.

Treatment

Dark quiet den, enforced rest, monitoring.

Dehydration

Occurs during heat waves or illness when fluid intake is insufficient.

Symptoms

Lethargy, dry mouth, weakness, sunken eyes.

Treatment

Wet moss, cool water, shade, rest.

Infection

Occurs when untreated wounds become inflamed and filled with bacteria.

Symptoms

Swelling, heat around wound, pus, fever.

Treatment

Marigold, burdock root, yarrow, cleaning the wound.

Night Terrors

A trauma-related mental condition.

Symptoms

Sudden waking, shaking, fear responses, refusal to sleep alone.

Treatment

Chamomile, lavender, reassurance, companionship.

Council Trials

ForsetiCh I

Half a cycle after Midsummer, Council member Hella falls ill. The healers conclude she was already carrying the plague when she attended the festival. A city-wide vote is held on whether to warn the other cities — the majority votes yes. Hella is quarantined in Sótt. She does not survive.

Shortly after, Hunter Liv returns from her mission delivering news to the other cities. She carries a message from Eldur: Forseti is forbidden from attending the coming Winternights celebration.

The Labyrinth

ForsetiCh I

The first generation is ready to graduate. Council members Ylva, Erica, Erling, and Ragna bring all current novices to the base of Helgafell for a coming-of-age trial — navigating the tunnels beneath the mountain, split into their pods.

  • Red PodHears unexplained crying that leads them through darkness. As they close in on the sound it stops entirely — the silence is absolute. The smell of grass and wind guides them out. The wind calls out their names as they exit.
  • Blue PodFeels watched. Shadows grow on the walls, following them. Growls and hissing are audible. A voice whispers "run." They find a large room with a strange glowing ditch — it dissolves with a hiss as they pass through. The wind calls their names at the exit too.
  • The meadowExiting, the novices find what looks like a flowery field — but underfoot, they hear crunching. The flowers are the bright wings of dead butterflies. Backward voices speak: "it to be heed my warnings or despair" / "beware he who is not… what he seems… not all is what you believe."

The Council members arrive, gasping. They call it a sign from the gods and usher the novices away. Any novice who looks back sees faint flickering lights — glowing eyes in the dark.

  • Ylva / Erica / Erling / RagnaCouncil members — supervised the trial; visibly shaken by what they found in the meadow
  • Arianrhod / Aslaug / Bengt / Colborn / Gleym / Lif / Misteltein / Rura / Syn / TusmørkeNovices navigating the tunnels
The backward voices, the dead butterfly wings, the glowing eyes — these are the first clear signs of the Hel Cult's presence in Forseti's territory. The council knows more than they tell the novices.
The phrase "beware he who is not what he seems" will take on new meaning as Forseti's traitor reveals themselves in Chapter II.

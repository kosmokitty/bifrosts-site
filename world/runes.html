Nordic Runes

Overview

In Norse Lore, the god Odin, impaled his heart with his own spear and hung on the world tree, Yggdrasil, for nine days and nights all to perceieve the meaning of the futhark runes. The runes were symbols born from the Well of Urd, the source of fate. These runes are not merely letters, but also have the potential to be of metaphysical or even magical nature.

These runes often predict what a cat will be like, known as their Birth Rune. The runestones may also be carved to commemorate ancestors, mark the graves of heros, and communicate between the natural and supernatural. Carving on sticks, bone, shells, or other objects, can cast these rune's special nature to discern the present or predict the future.

Birth Runes

Fehu

June 29 – July 14

Fehu is a celebration of prosperity and joy. Though Fehu literal translation is the ownership of cattle as a measurement of success, the underlying message of sustenance and comfort remains.

Uruz

July 15th – July 28th

Uruz signifies self-control, growth, and ascension to new changes with determination and persistence. In the context of romance, it signifies passion, harmony, and raw energy.

Thurisas

July 29th - August 13th

Thurisaz is a protective rune, it is the warning of temptation and adversity. Thurisaz is a warning to avoid dishonesty and trickery in trying to accomplish something and to remain cautious.

Ansuz

August 13th - August 29th

Ansuz refers to divine inheritance and the connection to the old gods. The Ansuz rune is for divine wisdom and power to flow into the human world from the old gods.

Raidho

August 30th - September 13th

Raidho is a rune of movement, it is representative of the human path. It is the idea of leading by examples, and the power of actions. Raidho is about the importance of life's journey as a whole.

Kaunan

September 14th - September 28th

This is the rune of the night, of inspiration. Kaunan represents knowledge and intellect, and the quest to seek these. This rune is for those that seek knowledge.

Gebo

September 29th - October 13th

Gebo symbolizes partnership and a balance between giving and receiving. Gebo predicts good fortune and discovering a gift that can come in many ways, whether material or emotional.

Wunjo

October 14th - October 28th

Wunjo is associated with joy, happiness, harmony, and the fulfillment of desires. It signifies a state of well-being and is linked to the satisfaction that's linked to achieving goals.

Hagalaz

October 29th - November 13th

Representing transformation and destruction, Hagalaz is the change from water to hail, and water again. The destruction and change lead to rebirth, resilience and a new way of being.

Nauthiz

November 14th - November 28th

This rune represents necessity, constraint, and hardship; it signifies the need for survival, resilience in the face of adversity, and the power of overcoming challenges.

Isa

November 28th - December 13th

Isa represents stillness and stagnation. When ice covers the land, everything becomes immobile. It might seem as though there is complete stillness, but there is growth.

Jera

December 14th - December 28th

Jera is the symbol of karma. This rune is about what you have been planting, and harvesting good fortune. This rune offers protection while you fight for your personal goals.

Eihwaz

December 28th - January 13th

Eihwaz indicates a time of transformation, which affects you mentally, spiritually, and physically. It's a symbol of time, age, death, endurance, and eternal life.

Perthro

January 14th - January 28th

Perthro is the symbol of mystery, fate (wyrd), and cosmic secrets. It is often associated with hidden knowledge, chance, unfolding destiny, and the unknown.

Algiz

January 29th - Feburary 13th

Algiz refers to the four elks that feed off the Yggdrasil. It is a rune that links divine consciousness, high spiritual awareness and protection.

Sowilo

February 14th - February 27th

Represented by the sun, this rune is a symbol of joy, abundance, and success; bringing light and energy to the world. It's a rune of good fortune, positivity, and celebration.

Tiwaz

February 28th - March 14th

Tiwaz is associated with justice, leadership, and sacrifice. This rune represents the god known as Tyr and symbolizes the qualities of a just, strong leader who's willing to make sacrifices for the greater good.

Berkanan

March 15th - March 30th

Representing new beginnings, this rune symbolizes a Birch tree. As that is the first tree to grow after the ice melts. It shows the connection to earth and new beginnings, and is the mother rune that carries all.

Ehwaz

March 31st - April 14th

Ehwaz represents movement and accepting the growth that comes to a person. It is a means of reflection and growth, accepting changes with grace and forging forward.

Mannaz

April 15th - April 29th

Mannaz is a symbol of self, community, and relationships. It's a reminder of our interconnectedness, our shared humanity, mutual respect, and empathy.

Laguz

April 29th - May 14th

Deeply connected to the water element, Laguz is the representation of endless possibility and spiritual connection. It is the rune of love, water, and travel; depicting the possibilites beneath the ocean.

Ingwaz

May 15th - May 29th

Ingwaz revolves around the themes of internal energy, personal growth, and transformation; just as a seed holds the potential for new life, but requires the proper time and nurturing.

Othala

May 30th - June 14th

Othala is the ancient rune of clan lands, and represents property and land that is strongly connected to the individual. It is an achievement at the end of the journey of runes.

Dagaz

June 15th - June 29th

Connected to daylight and inner self, Dagaz is about illumination and understanding, the sun breaking through barriers. It is night becoming day and symbolizes the end of trails and tribulations.
